"Very well," Regis said at last. "I see it is inevitable. It would appear I ought to have explained a few things to you long ago…"
"I ought to begin by saying," Regis said a moment later, "that all fears surrounding my vampire nature are groundless. I won't attack anybody, nor will I creep around at night trying to sink my teeth into somebody's neck.
I do not touch blood. Not at all and never.
I stopped drinking it when it became a problem for me. A serious problem, which I had difficulty solving."
The problem arose and acquired negative characteristics in true textbook style. Even during my youth I enjoyed... er... the pleasures of good company, in which respect I was no different to the majority of my peers.
With humans, however, there exists a system of rules and restrictions – parental authority, guardians, superiors, and elders – morals, ultimately.
We have nothing like that. Young vampires have complete freedom and exploit it. They create their own patterns of behavior. Stupid ones, you understand...
