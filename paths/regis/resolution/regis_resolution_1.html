Geralt turned his head away. (...) He smelled the intense scent of herbs and swore furiously.
"You're trying my patience, Regis."
The vampire, who had suddenly appeared out of thin air, was unconcerned, and sat down alongside the furious witcher.
Regis heaved a sigh, showing no intention of moving away.

"I was listening to your conversation with Dandelion and the archer," he said, not without a hint of mockery in his voice.

"I admit you've got a real talent for winning people over. Though the entire world seems to be out to get you, you disregard the comrades and allies wanting to help you."
"The world's turned upside down. A vampire's teaching me how to deal with humans. Why am I still talking to you?"

"The world's turned upside down," the vampire admitted. "You are talking to me indeed. Perhaps you'd also like to listen to some advice?"

"No. No, I wouldn't. I don't need to."

"True, I'd forgotten. Advice is superfluous to you, allies are superfluous, you'll get by without any travelling companions."
