“I promised to help you should you be in need. But, Milva, I can't help you.”

This time she remained silent. But he could not. He felt compelled to speak.

“It's impossible to repay something that has no price. Some say everything in the world has a price. It's not true.”
“There are things with no price, things that when you lose them, you lose them forever and nothing can get them back for you.

I have lost many such things. Which is why I can't help you today.”
“But you have helped me,” she replied, very calmly.

“You don't even know how.”
When they set off again at dawn, Milva rode at the head, calm and smiling.

And when Dandelion, who was riding behind her, began to strum away on his lute, she whistled the melody.
