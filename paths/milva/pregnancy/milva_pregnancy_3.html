“Milva—”
“Maria!  I’m Maria, not Milva.
What kind of Red Kite am I?

A mother hen with an egg, not a Kite…







Milva was treacherous, she led those people to their fate and laughed…
Milva laughed with the dryads on the battleground, pulled arrows from bloodied corpses. Waste of a good arrow shaft or a good arrowhead! And if someone was still breathing, a knife across the throat!
Now their blood calls. That blood, like a wasp’s venom, is devouring Maria from the inside.

Maria is paying for Milva.”
