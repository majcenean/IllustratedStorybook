“It’s a bigger problem than you think,” the surgeon said. “Or a lesser one. It all depends on one’s point of view.”

“I don’t understand.”
“She demanded,” Regis began a short while later, “that I prepare and give her a strong and powerful… medicament.

She considers it a remedy for the problem. Her mind is made up. I’ve already prepared the agent,” Regis said, showing them all a small bottle of dark glass.

“Should she ask again, I shall not refuse.

Should she ask again,” he repeated with force.
“What’s this all about then?
Unanimity? Total agreement?
Is that what you’re expecting?”
“You know very well what it’s about,” the surgeon answered. “You know perfectly well what ought to be done.

But since you ask, I shall tell you.

Yes, Geralt, that’s precisely what it’s about. Yes, that’s precisely what ought to be done.

And no, it’s not me that’s expecting it.”
