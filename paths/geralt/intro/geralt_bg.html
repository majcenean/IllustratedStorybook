The stranger was not old, but his hair was almost entirely white.

Beneath his cloak, he wore a worn black jacket of leather, studded with silver.

As he took off his cloak, those around him noticed that he carried a sword⁠ — not something unusual in itself, nearly every man in Vizima carried a weapon ⁠— but no one carried a sword strapped to his back as if it were a bow or a quiver.
He sheathed his sword behind his back and straightened the hilt above his right shoulder, then he tied a strip of leather across his forehead, pushing his hair behind his ears. He pulled on long gauntlets, bristling with short silver studs.

He looked at the sun, his pupils narrowed into vertical slits, and he thought to himself,
What a beautiful day. A beautiful day for a fight.
"Do you know him?"

"It's some albino," giggled a man. "Albino, mutant, freak of nature. And this sort of thing is allowed to enter pubs among decent people."
"People are saying he's a witcher."

"And what's a witcher?"

"A hired magician, a conjurer for a fistful of silver. I told you, a freak of nature. An insult to human and divine laws. They ought to be burned, the likes of him."
