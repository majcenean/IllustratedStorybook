"Do you know what your problem is, Geralt? You think you're different. You flaunt your otherness, what you consider abnormal.

You aggressively impose that abnormality on others, not understanding that for people who think clear-headedly you're the most normal man under the sun, and they all wish everybody was so normal.

What of it that you have quicker reflexes than most and vertical pupils in sunlight? That you can see in the dark like a cat? That you know a few spells. Big deal."
"And the fact that I'm saddened — the fact that I know I'm losing you — is cellular memory. The atavistic remnants of feelings in a mutant purged of emotion—"

"I can't stand it when you talk like that!" she exploded. "I can't bear it when you use that word. Don't ever use it again in my presence.

"Does it change the fact? After all, I am a mutant."

"There is no fact. Don't utter that word in front of me."
