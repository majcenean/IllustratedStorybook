"Shut it, you brat! And don't call me aunty."

"Come, come," Regis interjected placatingly. "Girls, take it easy. Milva, Angouleme, let there be concord. United we stand, divided we fall."
"It's impossible for the tracks to have been left by a horse (...)"

"When I say a horse, I meant a horse!"

Milva, as usual, preferred practice to theory. She dismounted and bent over, pushing her hat back on her head.

"The pup's right," she decided after a moment.
"It's like our darling Angoulême's catchphrase, which brims with youthful charm — "There comes a time when you've either got to shit or free up the shithouse. In other words—"

"Spare the other words. Those charming ones will suffice."
"Well, I never!" she said right after she'd been sat down in a chair and had her hands handcuffed behind the backrest. "Look what the cat's dragged in!"
"Oho," Regis said placidly. "Look what the cat dragged in."
"Angoulême, please.
There, eat some bread with patê."
"I have a problem... Geralt wants to leave me here when he goes back on the road. He says," she snapped, "that he can't put me at risk, and similar rubbish.
And I want to go with him, with Geralt, because it's only when I'm with him I'm not afraid that the coroner will catch me."
