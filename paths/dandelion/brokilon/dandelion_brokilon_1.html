“Once, they gave warnings. They shot an arrow into a tree trunk or into the ground, and that marked the border that you couldn’t cross.

If a fellow turned back right then, he could get out in one piece. But now it’s different.


Now they shoot to kill at once. (...) I’ll say it one more time:

If you don’t have to, don’t go there.”
“The whole point,”
he said, working hard to assume a resolute expression,
“is that I do have to.”
The years of practice paid off. The troubadour’s voice sounded beautiful and menacing, austere and cold. It rang with iron and valour.
