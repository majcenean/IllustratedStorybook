The bard adjusted his hat to a jaunty angle over his left ear, prodded his horse with his heels and headed into the ravine, whistling a well-known and extremely indecent cavalry song.

Dandelion quickened his pace in order to get away as quickly as possible. He didn’t want to sabotage the reputation he’d just earned himself.

And he knew, with his mouth drying up in terror, that he wouldn’t be able to whistle for much longer.
In his mind’s eye, he could already see bows bending, bowstrings being pulled back and sharp arrowheads being aimed at him.
He recalled watching shooting tournaments, competitions and archery displays as country markets. Straw targets and mannequins, studded or torn apart by arrowheads.
What does a man feel when he’s hit by an arrow? The impact? Pain? Or perhaps... nothing?
