The poet sat down and rested the lute on his knee. He plucked the strings, once then twice, then a third time. He twisted the pegs, turned the lute and began to play. And a moment later, to sing.
He didn’t hear — but he felt — somebody’s presence.

“Don’t shoot...” he whispered, obediently not looking around. “I come in peace...”

“Sh’aente.” [”Keep playing.”]
He obeyed, although his fingers had turned cold and numb on the strings, and he had difficulty making any sound whatsoever emerge from his throat. (...)

But he was a professional, dammit.
“I...” he began. “I am... The comrade of the witcher Geralt... I know that Geralt is among you in Brokilon. I have come...”
